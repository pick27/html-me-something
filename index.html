Scott Land - about me

General Info

I really truly hate being told to be creative. I prefer to have a reasonable blueprint of what is wanted. I want to be a web developer not a website designer.

I prefer to keep quite a bit of my life private. The picture on this about page is about 5 years old when I was about 50 pounds heavier.

I read lots of books. Luckily for me most of the books I have are either in Kindle or Nook format rather than actual physical books. The eight plus bookshelves of books is just the tip of the iceberg.

Employers and Jobs

Amazon - November 2015 to present. I am a fulfullment center associate. Where I work that means you move boxes from point A to point B in the warehouse. Recently most of my shifts at work have been as a splitter (either splitting packages as low side/high side/wrong line or as line X/line Y/wrong line). Almost all of the remaining shifts I am a scanner (scan package, move it to a pallet, scan other package on pallet to show it is on the right pallet). I prefer splitting since I don't have to constantly see how bad some pallets have been built.

USA800 - April 1987 to May 2014. This is a call center (mostly inbound). The first year I was one of the telephone operators. For the remaining time I was in the DP/IS/IT department. While learning about their database I was the one assigned to batch process credit cards, run late morning reports, and print/fax those reports. After learning the database and how they put scripts (what the operators see) together, I got to actually be a programmer.

Internal Revenue Service - January 1987. I was supposed to be a data transcriber on the night shift. My car and I found out we didn't like the hours so I quit quickly while still in training.

Northwest Missouri State University - Fall 1984 to Fall 1985. I was a Lab Consultant in the library's computer lab. I helped students with the equipment (printer/terminal/teletype) and sometimes with their programming assignments. I tried to ask them questions to get them going toward the solution rather than just telling them what to type.
